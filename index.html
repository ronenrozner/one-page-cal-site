How to Use The One-Page Calendar

This unique calendar design, based on an article written by Ethan Siegel titled " The one-page calendar that changes how you view the year " on BigThink, shows an entire year on one page, making it easy to find dates and days of the week without flipping through months.

  • Top center: The year (Leap years highlighted)
  • Top section: Months arranged by starting day
  • Left side: Days of the month (1-31)
  • Right side: Days of the week grid

üîç Finding Day of the Week

When you know the month and date:

  1. Find your month in the top section
  2. Find your date on the left side
  3. Trace down from month and right from date
  4. Where they intersect shows the day of the week
Example: For July 4th, find "July" at top, "4" on left, trace to intersection.

üìÖ Finding Specific Dates

When you know the month and day of week:

  1. Start at your month in the top section
  2. Move down to your desired day of the week
  3. Move left to see which dates fall on that day
Example: For Thanksgiving (4th Thursday of November), find "November", move to "Thursday", find the fourth occurrence.

üóìÔ∏è Finding Matching Months

When you know the date and day of week:

  1. Start at your date on the left
  2. Move right to your day of the week
  3. Move up to see which months match
Example: For "Friday the 13th", start at "13", move to "Friday", trace up to find matching months.

üí° Pro Tips

Month Days:
  • 31 days: Jan, Mar, May, Jul, Aug, Oct, Dec
  • 30 days: Apr, Jun, Sep, Nov
  • 28/29 days: February (29 in leap years)
Usage Tips:
  • Use two fingers when tracing paths
  • Take time to practice the method
  • Remember months are grouped by starting day
  • Much faster than traditional calendars!
