One-Page Calendar 2025

How to Use The One-Page Calendar

This unique calendar design, based on an article writen by Ethan Siegel named: "The one-page calendar that changes how you view the year" on BigThink, shows an entire year on one page, making it easy to find dates and days of the week without flipping through months. Here's how to use it effectively:

Understanding the Layout

  • Top page: The year (Leap years will be highlighted)
  • Top right: Months of the year (arranged by starting day of the week)
  • Top left: Days of the week (Sunday through Saturday)
  • Bottom left: Days of the month (1-31 in a standard calendar grid)

Finding What Day of the Week a Date Falls On

When you know the month and date:

  1. Place one finger on the desired month (top right)
  2. Place another finger on the desired date (bottom left)
  3. Slide the "month" finger down and the "date" finger right until they meet
  4. The day of the week where they intersect is your answer

Example: To find what day July 4th falls on, start with "July" at the top and "4" on the left, then slide until they meet at the day of the week.

Finding What Date a Specific Day Falls On

When you know the month and day of the week:

  1. Start at the desired month (top right)
  2. Move your finger down to the desired day of the week
  3. Move left to see which dates in the month fall on that day

Example: To find the fourth Thursday of November (Thanksgiving), start at "November," move down to "Thursday," then move left to find the fourth occurrence.

Finding Which Months Have a Specific Date/Day Combination

When you know the date and day of the week:

  1. Start at the desired date (bottom left)
  2. Move your finger right to the desired day of the week
  3. Move up to see which months contain this combination

Example: To find which months have a "Friday the 13th," start at "13," move right to "Friday," then move up to see the matching months.

Key Month Information to Remember

  • 31 days: January, March, May, July, August, October, December
  • 30 days: April, June, September, November
  • 28/29 days: February (28 in regular years, 29 in leap years)

Tips for Success

  • Use two fingers or hands when tracing from different starting points
  • The calendar works for the entire year – no need to flip pages
  • Remember that months are grouped by their starting day of the week
  • Take your time tracing the paths until the method becomes natural

With a little practice, you'll find this one-page format much faster than traditional month-by-month calendars!
